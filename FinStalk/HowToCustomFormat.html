Creating Custom SMS Formats 🛠️

Banks frequently change how they write their SMS alerts, which can sometimes cause the app to miss a transaction. With the Custom Formats feature, you can teach the app exactly how to read your bank's specific messages!

1. The Magic Tags

To create a format, you just take a real SMS from your bank and replace the changing details (like the amount or the date) with our special "Magic Tags".

Tag What it replaces in the SMS Example in SMS
[[amount]] The transaction amount 1047.00
[[account]] Your account or card number XX7007 or 1540
[[date]] The date of the transaction 26-Aug-24 or 2023-01-06
[[time]] The time of the transaction (if present) 09:50:52 or 14:29:37
[[to]] The merchant, person, or UPI ID you paid JIO PLATFORMS or Amazon Pay

🌟 The Wildcard: .*

If a bank adds random text at the beginning or in the middle of a message that you want to ignore, you can use a dot followed by a star .* to tell the app: "Ignore whatever text comes here."

Note on the end of messages: You do not need to type .* at the very end of your format! The app automatically ignores anything that comes after the very last character you type.

For example, if you type: Spent [[amount]] from [[account]] to [[to]]. Avl
The app will match everything exactly up to the word Avl, and then automatically ignore the rest of the message for you.

2. Step-by-Step Examples

Example 1: A Standard Debit (Money Spent)

Original Bank SMS:

HDFC Bank: Rs 1180.00 debited from a/c **7380 on 07-01-23 to VPA shailesh.saraf@okicici(UPI Ref No 300771677571). Not you? Call 18002586161

How you write the Custom Format:

  • Replace 1180.00 with [[amount]]
  • Replace **7380 with [[account]]
  • Replace 07-01-23 with [[date]]
  • Replace shailesh.saraf@okicici with [[to]]
  • Use .* at the beginning to ignore the bank name prefix.

Final Custom Format:

.* Rs [[amount]] debited from a/c [[account]] on [[date]] to VPA [[to]]

Example 2: A Standard Credit (Money Received)

Original Bank SMS:

Dear Customer, Payment of INR 45674.30 has been received towards your ICICI Bank Credit Card XX0006 on 22-FEB-23 through UPI. Thank you.

How you write the Custom Format:

.* Payment of INR [[amount]] has been received towards your ICICI Bank Credit Card [[account]] on [[date]]

Example 3: Short/Complex Formats

Original Bank SMS:

Rs. 304.00 debited from Airtel Payments Bank a/c Txn ID 638680169807 Call 180023400 for help

How you write the Custom Format:

Notice how this SMS doesn't have a date or account number? Just map the amount and the app ignores the rest!

Rs. [[amount]] debited from Airtel Payments Bank a/c

3. Important Tips for Success

  • Spacing matters: Ensure the spaces between your words match the SMS exactly.
  • Keep it simple: You do not need to capture every single word. Focus on the sentence that contains the amount, date, and account.
  • Experiment! If a format doesn't work the first time, check your spelling and try tweaking where you place the .* wildcard. Alternatively, you can always report the SMS using the app's "Report Missing Transactions" feature, and I will add official support for it within 24-48 hours!
